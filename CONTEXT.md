# Marketing Skills — Shared Language

This glossary defines terms used across all skills in this repo. When an agent loads any skill, this vocabulary applies.

## Core Concepts

**VoC (Voice of Customer)** — Verbatim language extracted directly from customers. The gold standard for copy research. Never paraphrase; preserve exact words.

**Message mining** — The process of extracting and categorising VoC from reviews, support tickets, transcripts, and intake forms.

**Verbatim language** — Exact words a customer uses, not a summary or interpretation. "Shooting pain down my leg" not "sciatica-type symptoms."

**JTBD (Jobs to Be Done)** — The functional and emotional outcome a customer is trying to achieve. Format: "Help me [action] [object] so I can [outcome]."

**Trigger event** — The specific moment or circumstance that made a prospect seek a solution *now* rather than later. Distinct from the underlying problem.

**Pain language** — Verbatim phrases describing the problem: physical descriptors, location, timing, intensity.

**Functional limitation** — What the customer *can't do* because of the problem. Reveals the hidden desire (the fantasy they're buying).

**Failed solution** — A treatment or approach the customer already tried. Reveals current beliefs and objections.

**Objection inventory** — A categorised list of every concern, fear, or hesitation customers express. Used to build FAQ sections and inline copy.

## Strategic Frameworks

**Awareness stage** — Where a prospect sits on Schwartz's 5-stage spectrum: Unaware → Problem Aware → Solution Aware → Product Aware → Most Aware. Determines what to say and how to say it.

**Market sophistication** — How fatigued a market is with marketing claims (Stages 1–5). Determines the type of messaging angle: claim → mechanism → identity.

**ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)** — Defines the market segment to target: demographics, psychographics, pain types, awareness stage distribution. In B2C healthcare, ICP = patient segment.

**Persona** — The individual decision-maker within the ICP. Includes name, age, occupation, JTBD, objections, communication preferences.

**Messaging hierarchy** — Prioritised list of messages and benefits ordered by customer importance and competitive differentiation.

**Gradualization** — Schwartz's technique: the step-by-step sequence of beliefs a prospect must accept to move from sceptical to convinced. Not content; sequence.

**Message-market fit** — When your messaging language maps directly to the words, concerns, and desires in your VoC data. The copy feels like it was written by the customer.

## Copy Structures

**PAS** — Problem → Agitate → Solution. Best for: emails, blog posts, short ads.

**AIDA** — Attention → Interest → Desire → Action. Best for: ads, landing page heroes.

**PASTOR** — Problem → Amplify → Story → Testimonials → Offer → Response. Best for: long-form sales pages.

**BAB** — Before → After → Bridge. Best for: email sequences, VSLs.

**4Ps** — Picture → Promise → Proof → Push. Best for: direct mail, sales letters.

## Domain: Healthcare / Chiropractic

**Intake form** — Patient-completed document before first appointment. Primary VoC research source. Contains raw pain language, failed solutions, functional limitations, and trigger events.

**PHI (Protected Health Information)** — Any patient data tied to identity. Must be stripped before AI analysis. Names, DOB, contact info, MRN.

**Compliance** — HIPAA (US) or Privacy Act (AU) rules governing patient data use in marketing. See `healthcare-copy` skill.

**Adjustment** — Chiropractic spinal manipulation. Use patient language in copy ("treatment," "adjustment"); avoid clinical jargon.

**Root cause** — The underlying structural/neurological issue vs. symptomatic relief. Core educational differentiator in chiropractic copy.
