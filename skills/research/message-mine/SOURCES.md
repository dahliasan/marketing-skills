# Message Mining — Source Guide

## High-Value Sources by Category

### Healthcare / Chiropractic

**Your own reviews**
- Google Business Profile — search: `[clinic name] reviews`
- Healthgrades.com — search: `[clinic name] [city]`
- RateMDs.com — same
- Yelp (US/AU) — filter by 3-star (most candid)

**Competitor reviews**
- Search: `chiropractor [city] reviews site:google.com`
- Search: `best chiropractor [city] reddit`

**Community forums**
- Reddit: r/Chiropractic, r/backpain, r/ChronicPain, r/sciatica
- Facebook: search "back pain [city]" in Groups
- Quora: "Is chiropractic worth it?" threads

**Q&A (objection goldmine)**
- Google: "Is chiropractic safe?", "How many chiro visits do I need?"
- Quora, Reddit answers to these questions

**Amazon review mining**
- Search: books on back pain, neck pain, sciatica
- 1-star and 3-star reviews reveal unmet desires and failed solutions

### Search Strings for Language Mining

```
site:reddit.com "chiropractor" "didn't work" OR "helped me" OR "wish I had"
site:reddit.com "back pain" "tried everything" OR "nothing works"
"google reviews" chiropractor [city] "been suffering" OR "finally" OR "years of pain"
healthgrades.com chiropractor [city]
```

## Spotting High-Value Phrases

High value (keep):
- Mentions a specific activity they can/can't do
- Has a time element ("for 6 months", "after my first visit")
- Expresses emotion (frustration, relief, scepticism, surprise)
- Compares to something else they tried
- Uses non-clinical language for clinical concepts

Low value (discard):
- "Great service", "Very professional", "5 stars"
- "Would recommend" with no context
- Mentions the practitioner's name only
- Single-word answers

## Volume Guidelines

| Source type | Minimum | Ideal |
|-------------|---------|-------|
| Your own reviews | All available | All available |
| Competitor reviews | 50 | 200+ |
| Reddit posts | 20 threads | 50+ threads |
| Survey responses | 20 | 100+ |
| Interview transcripts | 5 | 10-15 |

At 100+ responses, patterns become statistically meaningful. Below 20, treat findings as directional only.
